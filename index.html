VIRAT KOHLI

A Tribute to The RUN MACHINE

The Greatest batsman of All Time

Virat Kohli is a professional Indian cricketer and the former captain of the Indian national team. He was born on November 5, 1988 in Delhi, India. Kohli began his cricket career at a young age, playing for the West Delhi Cricket Academy. He quickly rose through the ranks and made his debut for the Delhi Under-15 team at the age of 14.

Kohli made his international debut for India in August 2008, playing in an ODI match against Sri Lanka. He quickly established himself as a top player, scoring his first century in 2009 against Sri Lanka. In 2010, he was named the vice-captain of the Indian ODI team and in 2013, he was named the captain of the team.

Kohli is widely considered to be one of the best batsmen in the world and is known for his aggressive and passionate style of play. He is the fastest player to reach 8,000, 9,000, 10,000 and 11,000 ODI runs. He has also scored the most centuries in chases in ODIs. In 2017, he became the first player in history to win the ICC ODI Player of the Year award for three consecutive years.


In 2020, he became the fastest player to reach 11,000 runs in ODIs and also became the fastest player to reach 7,000 runs in T20Is. He has also been named the ICC ODI Player of the Year three times (2012, 2017, 2018) and the ICC Test Player of the Year once (2017).
Off the field, Kohli is known for his philanthropic work and is actively involved in several charitable organizations. He is also a brand ambassador for several companies and has endorsed several products. He is considered one of the most marketable athletes in the world and is considered as one of the most followed cricketer on social media.

Overall, Virat Kohli is a true inspiration for many young cricketers and a role model for many. He has achieved a lot in his cricketing career and is still working hard to achieve more. He is considered as one of the best cricketers in the world and will be remembered for many years to come.
